What is investing?

In the pursuit of simplicity, one can say that to invest is to allocate money with the expectation of a positive return (profit) in the future. To explore this concept further you can visit: Wikipedia or Investopedia.

Taking into account the aforementioned definition, we can assume that there are different investing vehicles (assets or financial instruments) that one can choose.

In this website we will focus on investing in public businesses (commonly known as stocks).


What is a stock?

A stock is all of the shares (fractions) into which ownership of a corporation (business) is divided into. In other words, one can say that by owning a stock, one owns a fraction of a business.

If we own a great business (stock) that generates handsome profits, the stock price will follow. Conversley, if we own a bad business (stock) that loses money, the stock price will follow accordingly.

In that regard, there are different investing metrics (investing metrics) that we can use to evaluate, meassure the fundamentals of any public business that caught our attention.


What are the fundamentals of business?

The fundamentals are the intrinsic characteristics of the business. In simple words, its revenues, assets, liabilities, etc.

There are different metrics we can use to analyze the fundamentals of any public company. These metrics are ratios. A ratio is relationship between two concepts.
